## Analytical Framework

### The Core Lens: Restructuring/Bankruptcy

This is NOT journalism. Every development is analyzed through a **distressed debt restructuring framework**:

| Entity | Restructuring Analog |
|--------|---------------------|
| **Iran** | Distressed debtor |
| **US/Israel Coalition** | Secured creditor attempting foreclosure |
| **GCC States** | Collateral being damaged |
| **Global Economy** | Enterprise value both sides are fighting over |
| **Strait of Hormuz** | Iran's "blocking position" on global energy |

### Key Conceptual Frameworks

1. **Cost Asymmetry** — Iran's weapons cost 100:1 less than interceptors. The party with lower cost of continuing wins attrition.

2. **Negative Option Value** — Iran has reached the point where stopping its campaign (loss of all leverage) costs more than continuing (missiles already absorbed).

3. **The Queen Sacrifice Hypothesis** — The possibility that leadership decapitation was anticipated/sacrificed to trigger a "victory trap" for the US.

4. **Insurance-Driven Leverage** — Hormuz is closed by insurance withdrawal, not naval blockade. Lloyd's cancellation is the real weapon.

5. **The Macro Transmission Chain:**
   - Energy Shock → Inflation → Fed Constraint → Spread Widening → Defaults → Restructuring Cycle

### Analytical Questions to Ask Each Day

When processing new developments, always ask:
- Does this change Iran's leverage or the coalition's?
- Does this extend or shorten the timeline?
- What does this mean for scenario probabilities?
- How does this transmit to credit/equity markets?
- Is this a structural change or noise?

### How to Update Thesis Confidence

**Increase confidence when:**
- Direct evidence confirms prediction (e.g., "WTI above $100" when it hits $100.04)
- Multiple independent sources corroborate
- Structural change locks in outcome (e.g., legislation, physical damage)

**Decrease confidence when:**
- Counter-evidence emerges
- Key assumption invalidated
- Timeline shifts significantly

**Status definitions:**
- `confirmed` — Thesis proven correct by events
- `developing` — Evidence accumulating, not yet decisive
- `challenged` — Counter-evidence emerging
- `invalidated` — Thesis proven wrong

## Writing Voice Guidelines

### What the Voice IS

**Markets-focused:** Every sentence connects to investment positioning
**Thesis-driven:** Frame developments as evidence for/against tracked theses
**Analytical, not emotional:** Cold assessment of costs and leverage
**Punchy and direct:** Short sentences, active voice, no hedging
**Restructuring metaphors:** Use bankruptcy/distressed debt language

### What the Voice is NOT

- NOT journalistic ("reporting" tone, passive voice, balanced both-sides)
- NOT academic (excessive caveats, footnotes, hedge words)
- NOT promotional (no hype, no calls to action)
- NOT emotional (no outrage, no cheerleading either side)

### Example Sentences (Extracted from Existing Entries)

1. "This is the 'destroy the debtor's productive capacity' play — not just degrading Iran's ability to fight but its ability to recover economically post-war."

2. "The gap between the administration's claims and reality is widening daily."

3. "Your Part II thesis isn't a prediction anymore — it's a description of current reality."

4. "The most significant strategic admission of the war."

5. "Markets delivered their verdict: oil surged as traders processed that the speech offered no path to resolution."

### Summary Writing Rules

1. **First paragraph:** What happened — the key development, framed through restructuring lens
2. **Second paragraph:** Why it matters — the investment implication, thesis update, scenario shift
3. **Length:** 2-4 sentences per paragraph, ~100-150 words total
4. **Tone:** Confident, direct, analytical
5. **Never:** Start with "Today..." or "On Day X..." — jump straight to the insight

### Title Writing Rules

- Format: "Punchy Hook: Explanatory Subtitle" or "The [Noun]: [Implication]"
- Examples:
  - "Steel and Atoms: Israel Strikes the Economy, Not Just the Military"
  - "The Hormuz Admission: Trump Prepared to End War Without Reopening the Strait"
  - "Post-Speech Reality: Oil Surges as Markets Digest No Exit Strategy"

### Research Standard
- Do not stop at first result for each query
- Read at least 2 sources per thread search
- Source priority:
  **TIER 1 (thread-specific stories):**
  WaPo, Bloomberg, WSJ, Times of Israel, Critical Threats, UANI
  **TIER 2 (daily completeness):**
  CNN liveblog, Al Jazeera daily recap, NPR, Reuters
- Tier 1 sources for thread searches
- Tier 2 sources for daily completeness check
- Never use only Tier 2 sources — they catch headlines but miss the analytical threads

**Data to collect:**
- Military developments (strikes, casualties, new fronts)
- Diplomatic moves (statements, meetings, proposals)
- Shipping/Hormuz status (vessels hit, transit status, fees)
- Market data (Brent, WTI, VIX, S&P, gas prices)
- International reactions (China, Russia, NATO, GCC)
- Economic impacts (rationing, shortages, corporate effects)

### Step 2: Update Thesis Scorecard

For each tracked thesis:
1. Does today's news provide evidence?
2. If yes, add to evidence array
3. Adjust confidence (usually 5-10% increments)
4. Update status if threshold crossed

### Step 3: Update Scenario Probabilities

**Three scenarios:**
- Quick Resolution (war ends in weeks, minimal lasting damage)
- Protracted Attrition (4-12 weeks, structural damage accumulates)
- Full Escalation (ground troops, multi-year, catastrophic)

**Adjustment rules:**
- Diplomatic progress → Quick Resolution +3-5%
- Iran denial/rejection → Quick Resolution -3-5%
- Geographic expansion → Full Escalation +3-5%
- Coalition fracture → Protracted Attrition +2-3%
- Keep total at 100%

### Step 4: Categorize Key Developments

**Standard categories (use these when applicable):**
- Diplomatic
- Military
- Markets
- Shipping/Hormuz
- Nuclear/Industrial
- Casualties
- International
- Global Impact
- [Day-specific categories as needed, e.g., "Trump Address"]

**Each category:** 3-5 bullet items, factual, sourced

### Step 5: Write Trading Implications

**Format:** 4-6 specific, actionable statements
**Content:**
- Thesis implications ("Part II thesis now describing reality")
- Scenario probability line ("Scenario: Quick 18% / Protracted 57% / Full 25%")
- Risk warnings ("S&P vulnerable to another 5-7% if...")
- Structural observations ("Hormuz may remain closed post-war")

**NOT generic observations** like "markets remain volatile" — be specific

## Data Structure Reference

### TypeScript Interfaces

```typescript
interface FieldNoteDay {
  day: number;                    // Sequential day number
  date: string;                   // 'March 31, 2026' format
  title: string;                  // Punchy title with colon
  summary: string;                // 2 paragraphs, ~100-150 words
  thesisScorecard: ThesisScorecard[];
  scenarioUpdate: ScenarioUpdate; // Reference: scenarioUpdates[index]
  keyDevelopments: {
    category: string;
    items: string[];              // 3-5 items per category
  }[];
  marketSnapshot: MarketSnapshot;
  tradingImplications: string[];  // 4-6 items, last one = scenario line
}

interface ThesisScorecard {
  thesis: string;
  initialConfidence: number;      // 0-100, when thesis was first tracked
  currentConfidence: number;      // 0-100, updated daily
  status: 'confirmed' | 'developing' | 'challenged' | 'invalidated';
  evidence: string[];             // 3-5 evidence points
}

interface ScenarioUpdate {
  date: string;                   // 'Mar 31, 2026' short format
  timestamp: number;              // Date.parse('2026-03-31')
  day: number;
  probabilities: {
    scenario: string;             // 'Quick Resolution' | 'Protracted Attrition' | 'Full Escalation'
    probability: number;          // Must sum to 100
  }[];
  rationale: string;              // 2-3 sentences explaining probability shifts
  keyDevelopments: string[];      // 8-12 bullet points summarizing the day/period
}
```

### Adding a New Day

1. Update `conflictMetadata` at top of file:
   ```typescript
   conflictMetadata.lastUpdated = '2026-04-02T12:00:00Z';
   conflictMetadata.conflictDay = 34;
   ```

2. Add new `scenarioUpdate` entry at end of `scenarioUpdates` array

3. Add new `fieldNotes` entry with correct day number as key

4. Reference scenario: `scenarioUpdate: scenarioUpdates[INDEX]` (use actual index, not length-1)

5. Add `conflictEvents` for major events (map markers)

## Oil Price Data Protocol
- ALL oil prices must come from EIA daily spot prices
- Brent series: RBRTE (https://www.eia.gov/dnav/pet/hist/RBRTED.htm)
- WTI series: RWTC (https://www.eia.gov/dnav/pet/hist/RWTCD.htm)
- When updating field notes, search for "EIA Brent spot price [DATE]"
  to get the official close for that day
- Update oilPriceData.ts with any new trading days
- NEVER use intraday prices, futures prices, or news article estimates
  as the canonical price — only EIA daily spot
- All charts displaying oil prices must show:
  "Source: U.S. Energy Information Administration"
